  1. THE RECOMPENSE OF THE NONCHALANT‚õ™üë•

    1. Untimely Death:

      Ahab died before his time as a recompense of his carelessness towards God. Verse 34, 37. This is the fate that awaits other nonchalant. 1 Kings 21:19

    2. Slow and Painful Death:

      An unintentional and indirectional arrow was fired into the air and struck the nonchalant Ahab. God allowed him to experience a slow and painful death even as He (God) watched Ahab opposing Him. Verse 34-35.

    3. Helplessness:

      Those who are careless about God can't be cared for by others. Ahab died helplessly because it so happened that he was stricken as such that no man could help him. 1 Kings 22:20.

    4. Generational Destruction:

      The whole of Ahab‚Äôs generation were destroyed because of his carelessness towards God. Many families are faced with judgement of extinction except they repent and turn to God. 1 Kings 22:21, 2 Kings 10:11, 17, Prov. 15:27

    5. Loss of Opportunity:

      In those days, most of the power thrones were hereditary. The posterity of Ahab would have continued on the throne as kings but they lost it because of his nonchalance towards God. See Hosea 4:6
